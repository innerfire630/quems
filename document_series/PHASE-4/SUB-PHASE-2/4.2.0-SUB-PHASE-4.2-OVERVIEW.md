## 1. Sub-Phase Purpose

Sub-Phase 4.2 introduces the **operational controls** that an officer has over their counter and their notifications, and brings these together in the **full officer dashboard** that is the officer's home base inside the application.

Counter temporary closure is a critical real-world feature: an officer needs to be able to step away from their counter (for a break, a meeting, an emergency) without the system continuing to assign tickets to them, without customers waiting indefinitely, and without requiring an admin to intervene. The display board must reflect the closure within a second so customers know the counter is temporarily unavailable.

The notification toggle is a personal preference, completely independent of the counter state. An officer may want to disable push notifications while still keeping their counter open (so the counter continues to receive tickets, but the officer's mobile device stays quiet). Or they may want to enable notifications while their counter is temporarily closed (so they stay informed about queue activity even while on a break). The two states — counter open/closed and notifications enabled/disabled — are tracked in separate fields and enforced by separate endpoints.

The full officer dashboard is the **composition layer** where everything an officer needs comes together. It is not a re-implementation of any existing component; it imports and composes the `TicketActionPanel` from 2.3.2, the new `CounterStatusToggle` from 4.2.1, the new `NotificationToggle` from 4.2.2, and consumes the SSE infrastructure from Phase 3.1 via the `useSSE()` hook. The dashboard replaces the temporary stub page that Phase 2.3.2 put in place.

When this sub-phase is complete:

- An officer can mark their counter as temporarily closed with an optional reason (e.g., "On break", "In a meeting", "System issue").
- The closure is reflected on the display board within a second — the affected counter's card switches to the "Temporarily Closed" state.
- The closure is reflected on the officer's own dashboard within a second.
- A `CounterStatusEvent` record is created for every status change with the correct status, reason, and timestamps, providing an audit trail.
- A `COUNTER_CLOSED` (or `COUNTER_OPENED`) SSE event is emitted to the `global` channel (consumed by the display board) and to the `counter:[counterId]` channel (consumed by the officer's dashboard).
- An officer can toggle push notifications on or off for a specific counter. The toggle state is respected by the dispatch logic from 4.1.3 — when `notificationsEnabled = false`, no push notifications are sent to that officer's devices for that counter.
- The toggle is independent of the counter open/closed state — the two fields are tracked separately, updated by separate endpoints, and enforced by separate validation rules.
- The full officer dashboard at `/counter/[counterId]` shows the counter name, current serving ticket, queue depth, next ticket preview, Call/Recall/No-Show action buttons, counter status toggle, notification toggle, and recent activity feed.
- The dashboard updates in real time via SSE — when a ticket is queued, called, or marked no-show, when the counter status changes, when a notification is dispatched — the dashboard reflects the change within a second.
- A non-assigned officer cannot access another counter's dashboard (403).

Four problems are solved in this sub-phase, in order:

1. **The temporary counter closure system** — the `PATCH /api/counters/[counterId]/status` endpoint, the `CounterStatusEvent` audit record creation, the display board reflection of closure (via `COUNTER_OPENED` / `COUNTER_CLOSED` SSE events on the `global` channel), the dashboard reflection of closure (via the same events on the `counter:[counterId]` channel), the queue behavior during closure (tickets wait, no auto-reassignment, no new calls), the `<CounterStatusToggle>` UI component, and the Zod validation schemas. (Document 4.2.1.)
2. **The notification toggle feature** — the `PATCH /api/officers/me/notifications` endpoint, the `<NotificationToggle>` UI component, the independence rule between notification state and counter state (clarified in the master plan Section 4.2.2 and Section 8.4.3), the Zod validation schemas, and the audit log writes. (Document 4.2.2.)
3. **The counter officer dashboard** — the full officer dashboard layout replacing the Phase 2.3.2 stub at `app/(officer)/counter/[counterId]/page.tsx`, the composition with the `TicketActionPanel` (2.3.2), the `CounterStatusToggle` (4.2.1), the `NotificationToggle` (4.2.2), the `<QueueDepthIndicator>` and `<NextTicketPreview>` components, the `<RecentActivityFeed>` component, the `useSSE('counter:[counterId]')` subscription consuming ticket lifecycle and counter status events, the loading and empty state handling, and the route protection. (Document 4.2.3.)

When this sub-phase is complete, **Phase 4.2 is finished**. The officer has a complete operational surface: a counter they can open and close, a notification preference they can toggle, a live dashboard that shows their queue and lets them act on tickets, and a recent activity feed that records what happened. Sub-Phase 4.3 (officer reply, broadcasting, security screen) builds on top of the dashboard that 4.2 produces.

## 1.1 Why This Sub-Phase Comes After 4.1

Sub-Phase 4.2 depends on 4.1 in one critical way:

- **4.2.2 (notification toggle) writes to `CounterOfficer.notificationsEnabled`.** The dispatch logic in 4.1.3 reads this field to decide whether to send a push notification. Without 4.1.3, the toggle has no effect — the field exists but nothing consumes it.
- **4.2.3 (officer dashboard) shows the current notification toggle state.** The state is sourced from the `CounterOfficer` record that 4.1.3 queries during dispatch.
- **4.2.1 (counter closure) does not directly depend on 4.1**, but it emits `COUNTER_OPENED` / `COUNTER_CLOSED` SSE events that the dashboard (4.2.3) consumes. The events themselves are independent of the notification system.

Sub-Phase 4.2 produces the operational UI surface that the rest of Phase 4 builds on.

## 1.2 Why This Sub-Phase Comes After Phase 2 and Phase 3

Sub-Phase 4.2 cannot exist without Phase 2 and Phase 3:

- **Phase 2.3.1 (Call & Recall) is the source of the `TicketEvent` records** that 4.2.3's recent activity feed queries.
- **Phase 2.3.2 (No-Show)** creates the `TicketActionPanel` component that 4.2.3 reuses — no re-implementation.
- **Phase 2.3.3 (Daily Reset)** is unaffected by 4.2 (counter closure during a reset is a no-op since `Ticket.currentStatus` resets regardless), but the dashboard consumes the `DAILY_RESET` SSE event to clear its local state.
- **Phase 3.1 (SSE Infrastructure)** is the real-time transport that 4.2.3 subscribes to. The `useSSE()` hook from 3.1.2 is the consumption layer; the `broadcastEvent()` and `broadcastRoutedEvent()` functions from 3.1.3 are the production layer (4.2.1 emits counter status events).
- **Phase 3.2 (Display Board)** consumes the `COUNTER_OPENED` / `COUNTER_CLOSED` SSE events that 4.2.1 emits on the `global` channel. The display-state reducer from 3.2.3 already handles these event types — 4.2.1 does not need to modify the display board.
- **Phase 3.3 (Audio Announcements)** is not affected by 4.2 — counter closure does not trigger an audio announcement (the bell + TTS are for ticket calls only, not for status changes).

## 1.3 What This Sub-Phase Is NOT

- It is **not** a notification system. The notification infrastructure was built in 4.1. 4.2.2 only writes to a boolean field that 4.1.3 reads.
- It is **not** a mobile app. No Android or iOS code is written. The future Android app is a consumer of the toggle state via the `OfficerProfile.notificationsEnabled` field returned by the mobile login finalisation (4.1.2).
- It is **not** the officer reply flow. Phase 4.3.1.
- It is **not** the broadcast message system. Phase 4.3.2.
- It is **not** the security officer screen. Phase 4.3.3.
- It is **not** rate limiting. Phase 5.2.1.
- It is **not** PostgreSQL migration. Phase 5.3.
- It is **not** a new permission for the counter closure — `counter:close` is the same `counter:manage` permission that Phase 2.1.2 used for counter configuration, redefined more narrowly as "this officer can close the counter they are assigned to".
- It is **not** an admin override for permanent closure — permanent counter deactivation (setting `Counter.isActive = false`) remains an admin action via the counter management page (Phase 2.1.2). The temporary closure endpoint only toggles `CounterOfficer.currentStatus` between `AVAILABLE` and `CLOSED`.

### 2.3 The "Distributed Document" Principle

The user has explicitly asked to distribute the complexity across the three task documents. The split is:

- **4.2.1** owns the **counter closure backend and the `<CounterStatusToggle>` UI component**. It introduces the `PATCH /api/counters/[counterId]/status` endpoint, the `src/lib/counter-status.ts` module (centralised counter status operations), the `src/schemas/counter-status.schema.ts` Zod schemas, the `CounterStatusEvent` audit record creation, the SSE event emission (`COUNTER_OPENED` / `COUNTER_CLOSED` to both `global` and `counter:[counterId]` channels), the queue behavior during closure (no auto-reassignment, no new calls), and the toggle component. It does **not** own the dashboard composition (4.2.3 does), the notification toggle (4.2.2 does), or the FCM dispatch logic (4.1.3 owns).
- **4.2.2** owns the **notification toggle backend and the `<NotificationToggle>` UI component**. It introduces the `PATCH /api/officers/me/notifications` endpoint, the `src/lib/officer-notifications.ts` module, the `src/schemas/officer-notifications.schema.ts` Zod schemas, the per-counter toggle logic (officers with multiple counter assignments can have different preferences for each), the independence rule documentation, and the toggle component. It does **not** own the dispatch logic (4.1.3 reads `notificationsEnabled` but 4.1.3 already exists), the dashboard composition (4.2.3 does), or the counter closure logic (4.2.1 does).
- **4.2.3** owns the **full officer dashboard composition**. It introduces the full `src/app/(officer)/counter/[counterId]/page.tsx` page (replacing the 2.3.2 stub), the `src/app/(officer)/layout.tsx` with the simplified sidebar, the dashboard-specific components (`<QueueDepthIndicator>`, `<NextTicketPreview>`, `<RecentActivityFeed>`), the `src/lib/officer-dashboard.ts` server-side data loader, the `src/types/officer-dashboard.types.ts` types, and the SSE subscription wiring. It does **not** own the individual toggle components (4.2.1 and 4.2.2 do), the action panel (2.3.2 does), or any business logic — it is purely composition + orchestration.

An AI agent picking up any one of these three documents should be able to complete it independently, knowing only that the previous documents in the dependency chain are done.

## 4. Document Breakdown

### 4.1 Document 4.2.1 — Temporary Counter Closure System

**Owns:** The `PATCH /api/counters/[counterId]/status` endpoint, the `src/lib/counter-status.ts` module, the `src/schemas/counter-status.schema.ts` Zod schemas, the `CounterStatusEvent` audit record creation, the SSE event emission (`COUNTER_OPENED` / `COUNTER_CLOSED`), the queue behavior during closure (no auto-reassignment, no new calls to closed counters), the `<CounterStatusToggle>` UI component, and the route protection.

**Does NOT own:** The full dashboard composition (owned by 4.2.3), the notification toggle (owned by 4.2.2), the FCM dispatch logic (owned by 4.1.3), the call/recall endpoints (owned by 2.3.1), the no-show endpoint (owned by 2.3.2), the display board implementation (owned by 3.2).

**Key outputs:**

- `src/lib/counter-status.ts` — the centralised counter status module:
  - `async function getCurrentStatus(counterId): Promise<{ status, reason, lastChangedAt } | null>` — returns the latest counter status derived from any active `CounterOfficer` record with `isOnDuty = true` and their `currentStatus` field. If no officer is on duty, returns `null`.
  - `async function setCounterStatus({ counterId, officerId, newStatus, reason }): Promise<CounterStatusEvent>` — orchestrates the status change: validates the transition (e.g., cannot close an already-closed counter), updates the `CounterOfficer.currentStatus`, writes a `CounterStatusEvent`, returns the event. Wrapped in a single `prisma.$transaction` for atomicity.
  - `async function isCounterClosed(counterId): Promise<boolean>` — returns `true` if any active `CounterOfficer` record with `isOnDuty = true` has `currentStatus = CLOSED`. Used by the call/recall/no-show endpoints (via 2.3.1 / 2.3.2) to enforce the "no new calls to a closed counter" rule. The 4.2.1 document specifies the integration point; the actual enforcement happens in 2.3.1 and 2.3.2.
  - `async function getRecentStatusEvents(counterId, limit): Promise<CounterStatusEvent[]>` — returns the recent status events for the counter, ordered by `createdAt desc`. Used by the recent activity feed in 4.2.3.
- `src/app/api/counters/[counterId]/status/route.ts` — the PATCH endpoint:
  - `PATCH` handler. Authenticated via `auth()` + `withPermission(handler, PERMISSION_COUNTER_CLOSE)`. The `counter:close` permission is consumed here for the first time (defined as a system permission in Phase 1.3.1 but not used until 4.2.1).
  - Resolves the calling user. Requires them to be an assigned officer for the counter (the `CounterOfficer` record must exist for the calling user and the specified counter). If not, returns 403.
  - Validates the request body via `counterStatusChangeSchema` from `src/schemas/counter-status.schema.ts`. Body shape: `{ status: 'OPENED' | 'CLOSED', reason?: string (max 200 chars) }`.
  - Calls `setCounterStatus` from `src/lib/counter-status.ts`.
  - On success, emits the SSE event via `broadcastRoutedEvent('COUNTER_OPENED' | 'COUNTER_CLOSED', payload, { counterId })`. The `EVENT_ROUTING` entry is updated in `src/lib/events.ts` so both events route to `['global', 'counter:<counterId>']` (extending the 3.1.3 routing which only routed to `['global']`).
  - Writes an `AuditLog` entry with action `COUNTER_STATUS_CHANGED` AFTER the transaction commits (best-effort).
  - Returns `{ success: true, data: CounterStatusEvent }`.
- `src/schemas/counter-status.schema.ts` — Zod schemas for request bodies and types:
  - `counterStatusChangeSchema` — `{ status: enum(['OPENED', 'CLOSED']), reason?: string (max 200 chars, optional) }`. The `CLOSED` status requires the `reason` field — the `OPENED` status does not.
  - `counterStatusEnum` — re-exports the `CounterOfficerStatus` enum from `@prisma/client` for use in client components (the enum is auto-generated by Prisma; the schema file re-exports it for type consistency in the API surface).
- `src/components/counter/counter-status-toggle.tsx` — the UI component:
  - Client component (`"use client"`).
  - Props: `{ counterId: string; currentStatus: 'OPENED' | 'CLOSED'; reason: string | null; onStatusChange: (newStatus, reason) => void }`.
  - Renders a toggle/switch with a clear on/off state. When the officer clicks to close, shows a modal or inline form asking for the optional reason (with a sensible default like "On break").
  - On submit, calls the `PATCH /api/counters/[counterId]/status` endpoint with the new status and reason.
  - Shows loading state during the request. Shows success/error toasts.
  - Uses shadcn `Switch` or `Button` (depending on the visual style chosen). Uses shadcn `Dialog` or `Input` for the reason input.
- **`CounterStatusEvent` record creation** — happens inside `setCounterStatus`, in the same transaction as the `CounterOfficer.currentStatus` update. The event captures the full context: `counterId`, `counterOfficerId`, `status` (the new status), `reason`, `createdAt`.
- **SSE event payload structure** for `COUNTER_OPENED` / `COUNTER_CLOSED`:
  - `counterId: string`.
  - `counterNumber: number` — the `Counter.number`, denormalised.
  - `counterName: string` — the `Counter.name`, denormalised.
  - `status: 'OPENED' | 'CLOSED'` — the new status.
  - `changedByOfficerId: string`.
  - `changedByOfficerName: string` — denormalised officer name.
  - `changedAt: string` — ISO 8601 timestamp.
  - `reason: string | null` — only present for `COUNTER_CLOSED` (omitted from `COUNTER_OPENED`).
  - The payload shape is added to the `SseEventPayload` discriminated union in `src/types/sse.types.ts` (additive extension — the union is locked at 3.1, but additive variants are allowed).
- **`EVENT_ROUTING` extension** in `src/lib/events.ts`:
  - `COUNTER_OPENED: ['global', 'counter:<counterId>']` — replaces the 3.1.3 routing of `['global']`. Now routes to both channels.
  - `COUNTER_CLOSED: ['global', 'counter:<counterId>']` — same.
  - The context-based resolution (`<counterId>` placeholder) is handled by `broadcastRoutedEvent` (introduced in 3.1.3). The 4.2.1 endpoint passes `{ counterId }` as the context.
- **Queue behavior during closure** — enforced at the call/recall/no-show endpoints (2.3.1, 2.3.2):
  - The endpoints call `isCounterClosed(counterId)` BEFORE the transaction. If true, return 403 with code `COUNTER_CLOSED`.
  - The endpoints do NOT modify existing waiting tickets. The closure does NOT trigger any auto-reassignment.
  - The display board reducer (3.2.3) handles `COUNTER_CLOSED` by setting `counterStatus[counterId] = 'closed'`. The display card for the counter switches to opacity-50 with a "Temporarily Closed" label (already implemented in 3.2.1).
- **Audit log writes** — `COUNTER_STATUS_CHANGED` is a new value added to the `AuditAction` union (additive extension, per Phase 1.3.3 discipline). The `CounterStatusEvent` is the primary audit; the `AuditLog` captures the high-level event for the admin viewer.
- **No `broadcastEvent` calls from any 4.2.1 path except the status endpoint.** The component itself is client-side and does not emit SSE events. The endpoint emits via `broadcastRoutedEvent`.
- **No `Notification` table writes.** Counter closure does not trigger push notifications (4.1.3 is unrelated to counter status).
- **No `TicketEvent` writes.** Counter closure is not a ticket state change.
- **Route protection** — the `PATCH /api/counters/[counterId]/status` endpoint is guarded with `withPermission(handler, PERMISSION_COUNTER_CLOSE)`. The ownership check (calling user must be an assigned officer for the counter) is enforced inside the handler, AFTER the permission check.

**Master Plan sections implemented:** Sections 6.5 (`CounterStatusToggle` component), 8.2 (`CounterStatusEvent`, `CounterOfficer` model usage), 9.3 (status endpoint), 11.2 (event routing extension).

### 4.2 Document 4.2.2 — Notification Toggle Feature

**Does NOT own:** The FCM dispatch logic (owned by 4.1.3), the dashboard composition (owned by 4.2.3), the counter closure logic (owned by 4.2.1), the device registration endpoints (owned by 4.1.2).

**Key outputs:**

- `src/lib/officer-notifications.ts` — the centralised module:

  - Used by the dashboard's notification toggle UI to render the toggle with the correct initial state.
- `src/schemas/officer-notifications.schema.ts` — Zod schemas:
  - `officerNotificationsUpdateSchema` — `{ counterId: string (cuid format), notificationsEnabled: boolean }`.
  - No additional fields. The endpoint is intentionally minimal — the toggle is a single boolean write.
- `src/components/counter/notification-toggle.tsx` — the UI component:
  - Client component (`"use client"`).
  - Props: `{ counterId: string; counterName: string; notificationsEnabled: boolean; onToggle: (newValue: boolean) => void }`.
  - Renders a clear toggle/switch with a label like "Push Notifications for Counter X" or just "Push Notifications".
  - On change, calls the `PATCH /api/officers/me/notifications` endpoint.
  - Shows loading state during the request. Shows success/error toasts.
  - Uses shadcn `Switch`. Visual state: green/on when enabled, grey/off when disabled.
- **Independence rule** — documented in the doc and enforced by the data model:
  - `CounterOfficer.currentStatus` (AVAILABLE / CLOSED / SERVING / OFFLINE) — controlled by 4.2.1, reflects the counter's operational availability.
  - `CounterOfficer.notificationsEnabled` (boolean) — controlled by 4.2.2, reflects the officer's personal notification preference.
  - The two fields are tracked separately, updated by separate endpoints, and enforced by separate validation.
  - The dispatch system (4.1.3) checks `notificationsEnabled` before sending a push notification, regardless of `currentStatus`.
  - The call/recall/no-show endpoints (2.3.1, 2.3.2) check `currentStatus` before allowing the action, regardless of `notificationsEnabled`.

This is because an officer can be assigned to multiple counters, and the toggle is per-counter. The URL is `/api/officers/me/notifications` (not `/api/officers/me/notifications/[counterId]`). The counter ID is in the body to match the pattern of other toggle endpoints.

- **No SSE events emitted.** The toggle is a local preference change. The dispatch system reads the field on every dispatch — no need to broadcast.
- **No `broadcastEvent` calls from any 4.2.2 path.** The toggle does not need real-time fan-out.
- **Route protection** — the PATCH endpoint is authenticated (any authenticated user) and ownership-checked (the calling user must be assigned to the specified counter). No `withPermission` call.

### 6.4 Counter Status vs Notification Toggle Independence (NEW — Phase 4.2 specific)

The two toggleable states on a `CounterOfficer` profile are **strictly independent**:

- `currentStatus` (AVAILABLE / CLOSED / SERVING / OFFLINE) — controlled by 4.2.1, reflects the counter's operational availability.
- `notificationsEnabled` (boolean) — controlled by 4.2.2, reflects the officer's personal notification preference.

The rules:

- An officer can have `currentStatus = AVAILABLE` and `notificationsEnabled = false` (counter is open, but no push notifications).
- An officer can have `currentStatus = CLOSED` and `notificationsEnabled = true` (counter is closed, but they still get notifications — perhaps because they're on a break and want to know what's happening).
- The dispatch system (4.1.3) checks `notificationsEnabled` before sending, regardless of `currentStatus`.
- The call/recall endpoint (2.3.1, extended in 4.2.1) checks `currentStatus` before allowing the action, regardless of `notificationsEnabled`.

This independence is critical and is documented as a non-negotiable UX rule. The data model enforces it via separate fields. The endpoints enforce it via separate validation. The dispatch system enforces it via separate checks.

### 6.5 Queue Behavior During Closure (NEW — Phase 4.2 specific)

When a counter is marked as `CLOSED`:

- **Tickets that are waiting continue to wait.** No auto-reassignment to other counters. The customer experience is "this counter is temporarily unavailable; please wait for it to reopen or visit another counter in person".
- **No new calls can be made to the closed counter.** The call endpoint in 2.3.1 returns a 403 error with code `COUNTER_CLOSED` if the counter is closed. The no-show endpoint in 2.3.2 behaves the same way for tickets that were called before the closure (the officer can still mark them as no-show after the grace period).
- **The display board reflects the closure** within 1 second via the `COUNTER_CLOSED` SSE event on the `global` channel. The display card for the counter switches to opacity-50 with a "Temporarily Closed" label (already implemented in 3.2.1).
- **In-progress tickets (CALLED, RECALLED, SERVING) at the moment of closure** are unaffected. The officer can still recall or mark them as no-show. The closure only prevents NEW tickets from being called to the counter.

### 6.6 Audit Logging Standard (Sub-Phase 4.2 Specific)

- `COUNTER_STATUS_CHANGED` (4.2.1, every status change) — written to `AuditLog`.
- `NOTIFICATIONS_TOGGLED` (4.2.2, every toggle change) — written to `AuditLog`.
